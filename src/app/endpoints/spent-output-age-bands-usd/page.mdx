# Spent Output Age Bands (USD)

The **Spent Output Age Bands (USD)** metric distributes spending activity across age cohorts in dollar values, showing the economic scale of Bitcoin spending by different holder time horizons. {{ className: 'lead' }}

This metric reveals the absolute economic impact of each holder cohort's spending activity, enabling analysis of distribution intensity and capital flows in normalized dollar terms.

---

## Endpoint

`GET /spent-output-age-bands-usd`

**Base URL:**  
`https://api.blocklytics.net/api/public/v1`

### Interpretation

**High short-term dollar values** indicate substantial economic activity from recent holders and traders, revealing significant capital turnover without long-term conviction, typical during volatile periods when speculation drives large dollar volumes.

**High long-term dollar values** demonstrate major economic distribution from veteran holders, revealing significant capital that survived cycles is exiting, often marking cycle tops when billions in patient capital decide to realize gains.

**Rising long-term dollar spending during rallies** warns of accelerating veteran holder distribution at scale, indicating growing dollar amounts of experienced capital exiting positions, often preceding major corrections when this supply overwhelms demand.

**Record long-term dollar spending** signals unprecedented distribution from committed holders, often marking generational cycle peaks when the largest ever dollar amounts of patient capital accumulated over years finally exits markets.

### Use Cases

This metric provides dollar-normalized insight into the economic scale of spending activity across holder cohorts, enabling meaningful comparison across different price environments and market cycles. The USD denomination reveals the actual economic impact of each cohort's spending, distinguishing between token volume movements and genuine capital flows. Large dollar values from long-term bands indicate economically significant distribution events where substantial capital accumulated over years enters circulation, carrying more market impact than equivalent BTC amounts from recent holders. Tracking long-term band dollar spending relative to market capitalization contextualizes distribution intensity, with high ratios indicating veteran holders are releasing meaningful portions of network value. Historical comparison of dollar spending identifies extreme events, with record-breaking long-term spending often marking major cycle peaks when unprecedented capital decides to exit. The metric also reveals cycle maturity through spending patterns, with early bull markets showing modest long-term dollar spending while late stages show explosive growth as veterans capitalize on gains. Comparing dollar spending growth rates across bands identifies which cohorts are accelerating activity, with disproportionate long-term growth warning of distribution intensification. The absolute dollar values enable assessment of actual selling pressure magnitude, as large long-term band spending indicates substantial supply entering markets regardless of percentage contributions. Monitoring total spending across all bands in dollar terms reveals overall market liquidity and activity levels, with elevated totals indicating high transaction velocity while depressed values suggest illiquidity.

```js {title="Node.js (Express) example"}
// Example Express route using Blocklytics
router.get('/spent-output-age-bands-usd', async (req, res) => {
  try {
    const window = req.query.window || 'day'
    const from = req.query.from ? `&from=${req.query.from}` : ''
    const to = req.query.to ? `&to=${req.query.to}` : ''
    const limit = req.query.limit ? `&limit=${req.query.limit}` : ''
    const format = req.query.format ? `&format=${req.query.format}` : ''

    const path = `spent-output-age-bands-usd?window=${window}${from}${to}${limit}${format}`
    const data = await blocklyticsFetch(path)

    if (!Array.isArray(data)) {
      return res.status(500).json({ error: 'Unexpected API format', data })
    }

    const formatted = data.map((d) => ({
      date: d.date,
      range_0d_1d_usd: d.range_0d_1d_usd,
      range_1d_1w_usd: d.range_1d_1w_usd,
      range_1w_1m_usd: d.range_1w_1m_usd,
      range_1m_3m_usd: d.range_1m_3m_usd,
      range_3m_6m_usd: d.range_3m_6m_usd,
      range_6m_12m_usd: d.range_6m_12m_usd,
      range_12m_18m_usd: d.range_12m_18m_usd,
      range_18m_2y_usd: d.range_18m_2y_usd,
      range_2y_3y_usd: d.range_2y_3y_usd,
      range_3y_5y_usd: d.range_3y_5y_usd,
      range_5y_7y_usd: d.range_5y_7y_usd,
      range_7y_10y_usd: d.range_7y_10y_usd,
      range_10y_inf_usd: d.range_10y_inf_usd,
    }))
    res.json(formatted)
  } catch (err) {
    res.status(500).json({ error: err.message })
  }
})
```
