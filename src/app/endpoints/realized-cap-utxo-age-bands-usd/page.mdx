# Realized Cap - UTXO Age Bands (USD)

The **Realized Cap UTXO Age Bands (USD)** metric distributes realized capitalization across age cohorts in absolute dollar values, showing the scale of capital held by different holding periods. {{ className: 'lead' }}

This metric reveals the absolute economic weight of different holder cohorts rather than relative percentages, enabling analysis of capital flows and cohort size changes over time.

---

## Endpoint

`GET /realized-cap-utxo-age-bands-usd`

**Base URL:**  
`https://api.blocklytics.net/api/public/v1`

### Interpretation

**Growing short-term band values** indicate substantial fresh capital entering at current prices, revealing the absolute scale of new investment and potential supply at risk if sentiment shifts, with larger values indicating more significant recent accumulation or distribution.

**Growing long-term band values** demonstrate successful aging of capital into committed holder cohorts, showing absolute capital amounts that have survived volatility and established conviction, indicating strengthening fundamental support with less mobile supply.

**Declining long-term band values** signal distribution from established holders as older coins move, revealing the absolute scale of capital exiting strong-hand positions, often marking major supply releases during bull markets when long-term holders take profits.

**Rapid increases in multiple bands** suggest widespread capital rotation across the holder base, indicating significant market activity and turnover that can precede major directional moves as supply restructures and new equilibriums form.

### Use Cases

This metric provides absolute dollar-denominated insight into holder cohort sizes, complementing the percentage distribution by revealing actual capital scale rather than relative proportions. The absolute values enable tracking of real capital flows, showing whether specific age bands are growing or shrinking in dollar terms regardless of overall market cap changes. During bull markets, monitoring absolute growth in short-term bands quantifies the scale of new capital entering at elevated prices, revealing whether rally breadth is expanding with genuine new investment or narrowing as only existing capital churns. The absolute values also enable cohort growth rate analysis, comparing how quickly different age bands accumulate or shed capital to identify which holder types are driving market dynamics. Tracking the absolute scale of long-term bands reveals the dollar amount of committed capital that has demonstrated strong-hand behavior, providing a measure of supply stability and resistance to volatility. When long-term band values decline substantially in absolute terms during bull markets, it signals major profit-taking from established holders releasing meaningful supply, while stable or growing values during rallies indicate strong hands maintaining positions despite tempting gains. The metric also enables comparison across market cycles, showing whether current holder cohort structures differ from previous cycles in absolute capital scale, which has implications for supply dynamics and potential volatility. Analyzing the ratio between short-term and long-term absolute values reveals capital concentration patterns, with high short-term to long-term ratios indicating fresh capital dominance while low ratios suggest veteran holder dominance.

```js {title="Node.js (Express) example"}
// Example Express route using Blocklytics
router.get('/realized-cap-utxo-age-bands-usd', async (req, res) => {
  try {
    const window = req.query.window || 'day'
    const from = req.query.from ? `&from=${req.query.from}` : ''
    const to = req.query.to ? `&to=${req.query.to}` : ''
    const limit = req.query.limit ? `&limit=${req.query.limit}` : ''
    const format = req.query.format ? `&format=${req.query.format}` : ''

    const path = `realized-cap-utxo-age-bands-usd?window=${window}${from}${to}${limit}${format}`
    const data = await blocklyticsFetch(path)

    if (!Array.isArray(data)) {
      return res.status(500).json({ error: 'Unexpected API format', data })
    }

    const formatted = data.map((d) => ({
      date: d.date,
      range_0d_1d_usd: d.range_0d_1d_usd,
      range_1d_1w_usd: d.range_1d_1w_usd,
      range_1w_1m_usd: d.range_1w_1m_usd,
      range_1m_3m_usd: d.range_1m_3m_usd,
      range_3m_6m_usd: d.range_3m_6m_usd,
      range_6m_12m_usd: d.range_6m_12m_usd,
      range_12m_18m_usd: d.range_12m_18m_usd,
      range_18m_2y_usd: d.range_18m_2y_usd,
      range_2y_3y_usd: d.range_2y_3y_usd,
      range_3y_5y_usd: d.range_3y_5y_usd,
      range_5y_7y_usd: d.range_5y_7y_usd,
      range_7y_10y_usd: d.range_7y_10y_usd,
      range_10y_inf_usd: d.range_10y_inf_usd,
    }))
    res.json(formatted)
  } catch (err) {
    res.status(500).json({ error: err.message })
  }
})
```
